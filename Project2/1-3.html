Once upon a                             while I pondered
midnight dreary,                           weak and weary,
Over many a                                 volume of
quaint and curious                         forgotten lore -
While I nodded,                                suddenly
nearly napping,                          there came a tapping,
As of some one                               rapping at
gently rapping,                          my chamber door.
"'Tis some visiter,"                        "tapping at
I muttered,                               my chamber door
- Only this                                and nothing more."
Ah, distinctly                                   it was in
I remember                               the bleak December;
And each separate                            dying ember
wrought its ghost                          upon the floor.
Eagerly I wished                         - vainly I had sought
the morrow;                                    to borrow
From my books                                  sorrow
surcease of sorrow -  for the lost Lenore -
For the rare                                 and radiant maiden
whom the angels                            - Nameless here
name Lenore                                 for evermore.
